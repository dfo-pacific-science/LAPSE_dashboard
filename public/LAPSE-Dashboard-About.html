Welcome to the LAPSE Dashboard

The Legislation Applicable to Pacific Salmon and Ecosystems (LAPSE) Dashboard allows you to explore Canadian federal and British Columbian provincial legislation relevant to Pacific salmon stewardship. This tool bridges the gap between legal language and natural resource management by mapping legislation to internationally recognized biodiversity threat categories (IUCN-CMP threats) through a novel system of the management domain framework.

This dashboard allows you to filter and explore legislation by management domain, jurisdiction, and specific acts or regulations—making it easier to understand the regulatory landscape governing Pacific salmon.

To learn more about the creation of management domains and the legislative review process, please refer to the LAPSE Technical Brief linked below.

How to Use the LAPSE Dashboard

Getting Started

  1. Select a Management Domain - On the left sidebar, click on a management domain (e.g., Spatial Designation, Pollution, Fisheries) to filter legislation by that topic. Each domain shows the number of relevant sections in brackets. Click "All Domains" to reset.
  2. Filter by Jurisdiction - Use the toggle buttons to view Federal, Provincial, or All legislation.
  3. Search for Keywords - Enter a word or phrase in the Search Keywords box to find specific content across all legislation.
  4. Select Legislation - Choose a specific Act from the dropdown menu, then optionally select a Regulation, Code, or Order under that Act.
  5. Apply Clause Filters - Use the Clause Filters panel on the right to narrow results by Clause Type, Actionable Type, Responsible Official, or Discretion Type.
  6. Explore Sections - Click on any section to expand it and read the full legislative text. Keywords will be highlighted for easy reference. Keywords highlighted in red are associated with IUCN Threats, while keywords highlighted in blue are associated with Clause Types.

Key Features

  • Management Domains: Categories representing administrative responsibilities for Pacific salmon (e.g., Spatial Designation, Pollution, Water Use and Watercourse Modifications, Fisheries, Governance Administration, Restoration, Species Status and Assessment, Forest and Range, Transportation Infrastructure, Invasive or Problematic Species and Disease, and more)
  • Dual Jurisdiction View: Toggle between Federal and Provincial legislation, or view both simultaneously
  • Keyword Search: Full-text search across all collected legislation with highlighted matches
  • Hierarchical Navigation: Browse by Act, then drill down to specific Regulations, Codes, or Orders
  • Clause Filters: Filter results by Clause Type, Actionable Type, Responsible Official, and Discretion Type
  • Interactive Visualizations: Toggle between Keywords, IUCN-CMP threats, and Clause Type views to see distributions for your selected filters
  • Collapsible Sections: Expand only the sections you want to read for a cleaner viewing experience
  • Direct Links: Click "View Full Legislation" to access the complete official text on government websites
  • Data Export: Download filtered results as an XLSX file using the Download button in the Results & Visualizations panel
Tip: Start with a Management Domain that interests you, then use the keyword search to find specific topics. The visualizations on the right will update dynamically to show you the distribution of content related to your selection.

Understanding the Visualizations

Toggle between three visualization types in the Results & Visualizations panel:

  • Keywords: Shows the most common keywords in your filtered results, helping you understand the main themes
  • IUCN: Displays which IUCN threat categories appear in your selected management domain or legislation
  • Clause Type: Shows the distribution of clause types (e.g., prohibitions, definitions, penalties) in your selection

Clause Filters

Refine your search using the Clause Filters panel:

  • Clause Type: Filter by the type of legal clause (e.g., prohibition, requirement, definition)
  • Actionable Type: Filter by the nature of the action described
  • Responsible Official: Filter by which official or authority is responsible
  • Discretion Type: Filter by the level of discretion granted in the legislation
